# Provenance Guard — Planning & Spec

Design document written before implementation. This is the canonical spec and the primary prompting context for the build milestones.

---

## Problem

Creative-sharing platforms need to tell readers whether a posted piece was written by a human or generated by AI — not to police creativity, but to protect attribution and build trust. Perfect AI detection is an unsolved problem, so the system must communicate *uncertainty honestly* and give creators a path to appeal rather than pretending to a binary truth.

**Core design bias:** a false positive (labeling a human's work as AI) is worse than a false negative on a writing platform. The scoring thresholds and label wording are tuned to avoid wrongly accusing humans.

---

## Architecture

### Component map

| Component | Responsibility |
|-----------|----------------|
| `app.py` | Flask app; routes `POST /submit`, `POST /appeal`, `GET /log` |
| `signals.py` | `llm_signal()` (Groq semantic) + `stylometry_signal()` (structural) |
| `scoring.py` | combine two signal scores → calibrated confidence → attribution → transparency label |
| `audit.py` | structured audit log (SQLite); read/write decisions and appeals |
| `.env` | `GROQ_API_KEY` (gitignored) |

### Submission flow

```
                 raw text + creator_id
                          │
                   POST /submit
                          │
        ┌─────────────────┴─────────────────┐
        ▼                                    ▼
  llm_signal(text)                  stylometry_signal(text)
  Groq → ai_score 0..1              variance/TTR/punct → ai_score 0..1
        │   (llm_score)                      │   (stylo_score)
        └─────────────────┬─────────────────┘
                          ▼
              scoring.combine(llm, stylo)
          ai_score → attribution + confidence
                          │  (combined score)
                          ▼
                 label_for(attribution, confidence)
                          │  (label text)
                          ▼
                  audit.write(entry)   ──►  SQLite
                          │
                          ▼
   response: {content_id, attribution, confidence, label}
```

### Appeal flow

```
   content_id + creator_reasoning
              │
        POST /appeal
              │
   audit.file_appeal(content_id, reasoning)
     status: "classified" → "under_review"
     append appeal_reasoning to the entry
              │
              ▼
   response: {content_id, status: "under_review", message}
```

**Narrative:** A submission's text is scored independently by two signals — a semantic LLM check and a structural stylometry check — whose AI-probability scores are combined into one calibrated confidence. That confidence selects one of three transparency labels and the whole decision is written to the audit log before the response returns. An appeal references the original `content_id`, flips its status to `under_review`, and records the creator's reasoning alongside the original decision; no automatic re-classification happens.

## Detection signals (2 distinct)

The two signals are genuinely independent: one is **semantic**, one is **structural**. Combining them is more informative than either alone.

### Signal 1 — LLM classification (Groq, `llama-3.3-70b-versatile`)

- **Measures:** semantic and stylistic coherence holistically — does the text *read* like AI? Captures things like generic hedging, even tone, lack of lived specificity.
- **Why it differs human vs AI:** LLMs trained on human text have learned what AI "tells" look like; they assess meaning, not just surface stats.
- **Output:** a float 0–1 = P(AI), parsed from a structured model response.
- **Blind spot:** can be fooled by lightly-edited AI text or by formal human writing that mimics AI cadence; non-deterministic; costs an API call; can hallucinate confidence.

### Signal 2 — Stylometric heuristics (pure Python)

- **Measures:** statistical structure of the prose:
  - **Sentence-length variance (burstiness)** — humans vary sentence length a lot; AI tends to be uniform. Low variance → more AI-like.
  - **Type-token ratio (vocabulary diversity)** — measured per fixed window; very even, mid-range diversity reads AI-like.
  - **Punctuation density** — humans use irregular punctuation (dashes, ellipses, parentheticals); AI is more even.
- **Why it differs:** AI text is structurally more uniform; human writing is more variable ("bursty").
- **Output:** a float 0–1 = P(AI), combined from the three metrics.
- **Blind spot:** short texts give unreliable statistics; formal/edited human writing (essays, published prose) can look uniform and score AI-like; poetry with heavy repetition misleads it.

---

## False-positive trace (the scenario that drives the design)

A human writes a polished, formal essay. Stylometry sees low burstiness and even punctuation → high stylo AI-score. If the LLM signal is only mildly suspicious, the combined score lands in the **0.40–0.72 band**.

- The system does **not** call it AI. Asymmetric thresholds keep it in **"uncertain."**
- The transparency label says the system isn't sure — it does not accuse the writer.
- Confidence is reported as *low*, signaling the verdict is weak.
- The creator can `POST /appeal` with their reasoning; status flips to `under_review` and a human can examine it.

This trace is why the AI threshold is set higher than the human threshold, and why "uncertain" is a first-class outcome rather than a coin-flip at 0.5.
